# Business Problem Definition

## Core Business Problem

The business appears revenue-efficient at the surface level, but operational and profitability performance may be deteriorating underneath due to discount dependency, delivery inefficiency, and uneven SKU contribution.

High-revenue products may create the illusion of business health while contributing disproportionately to operational drag, delayed fulfillment, and weak profit generation.

The central risk is not declining revenue.

The central risk is scaling operational inefficiency under the appearance of commercial growth.

---

## Core Business Questions

1. Which SKUs generate high revenue but weak or negative profit contribution?

2. Are delivery delays and operational inefficiencies concentrated among specific products, regions, or shipping modes?

3. Does discount dependency systematically align with weaker profitability outcomes?

4. Which products behave as operational profit traps despite appearing commercially successful?

5. How exposed is the business to operational stress under continued revenue growth patterns?

---

## Intended Analytical Outcome

The project aims to identify where revenue performance diverges from operational and profitability health, allowing decision-makers to distinguish between true business contribution and misleading commercial performance.

---

## Analytical Constraints

This project uses transactional order data rather than direct ERP or warehouse inventory systems.

As a result:
- Inventory-related metrics are modeled estimates
- Supplier-level conclusions are avoided
- Operational findings are based on transactional and fulfillment behavior patterns
- Correlation is not treated as causation without explicit statistical support
